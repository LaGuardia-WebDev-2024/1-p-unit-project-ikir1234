National Parks in the USA

History

The National Park Service was established on August 25, 1916, through an act signed by then-president Woodrow Wilson and other founders that include Theodore Roosevelt and John Muir. The first national park, however, was Yellowstone National Park and was designated National Park on March 1, 1872 by Ulysses S. Grant.

Today, there are more than 431 units that include national monuments, national sites, national reserves, etc. However, there are 63 sites that are designated National Park.

In this website, I will be going over a few I have been to and ones I wish to go to.

Olympic National Park

Located in the Northwestern region in the Olympic Penninsula of Washington state, Olympic National Park was established in 1938 and is said to be one of the most diverse among National Parks. The park has a coastline by the Pacific Ocean, the Hoh Rainforest, tundras, and the Olympic Mountain Range.

Because of the diverse amount of ecosystems and terrain at Olympic National Park, it also holds a unique community of animals. Offshore, is the Pacific Ocean that is home to dolphins, sea otters, sea lions, seals, and the occasion whales as well. On land, animals found include beavers, racoons, minks, elks, cougars, and bears.

Grand Canyon National Park

One of the most iconic National Parks in the USA, the Grand Canyon National Park is located in the northern region of Arizona and is a total length 277 miles. The Grand Canyon is shaped the way it is today because of the Colorado River, the 5th longest river in the US, spanning a total of 1,450 miles.
