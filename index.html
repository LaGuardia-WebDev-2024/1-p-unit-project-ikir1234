National Parks in the USA

History

The National Park Service was established on August 25, 1916, through an act signed by then-president Woodrow Wilson and other founders that include Theodore Roosevelt and John Muir. The first national park, however, was Yellowstone National Park and was designated National Park on March 1, 1872 by Ulysses S. Grant.

Today, there are more than 431 units that include national monuments, national sites, national reserves, etc. However, there are 63 sites that are designated National Park.
